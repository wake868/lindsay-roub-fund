Lindsay’s Story

On November 1, 2013 Lindsay and her boyfriend decided to take advantage of the last day of hunting season in Scappoose, Oregon. They had literally been in the woods about ten minutes when the unfortunate accident took place. As the pair made their way down a steep embankment Lindsay started to lose her footing. She asked her boyfriend, who was ahead of her, to take her weapon, a 7mm Magnum. As the rifle exchanged hands, the barrel hit the ground and fired hitting Lindsay’s inner thigh. Disbelief came over the couple as they realized what had just occurred. Her boyfriend quickly removed his belt and used it as a tourniquet. Having no cell reception, he told Lindsay he would have to leave her to call for help. “ I was terrified as I layed there alone in the woods, holding the belt tight that was wrapped around my leg. I kept telling myself not to let go of the belt. I thought for sure I was going to die or lose my leg”, Lindsay recalls. Help arrived approximately 20-30 minutes later and Lindsay was transferred by ambulance to the hospital. Her family and friends then learned that the bullet had traveled up her leg into her pelvis and abdomen, breaking her hip, damaging her colon and lacerating her liver.

Miraculously, after six surgeries and almost three weeks in the hospital, Lindsay did not lose her leg and is expected to make a full recovery! However, she will be unable to return to work for 3-4 months and as you can imagine will have substantial medical expenses.

Let's help Lindsay by taking some of the financial stress off of her during this very long recovery process.
